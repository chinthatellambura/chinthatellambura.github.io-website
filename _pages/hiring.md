## Guidelines for Prospective MSc/Ph.D. Students

- Thank you for your interest in my research, ECE, and the University of Alberta.

- I typically recruit a few students each year.

- A typical student I would recruit would have most (not necessarily all) of the following traits:

  - Top GPA at one of their country's top universities, and other strong academic credentials. A high GRE score is also important, but not critical. You must also satisfy the English language requirements (see links below).

  - BSc/MSc degrees in Electrical Engineering, Mathematics, or Physics.

  - A strong aptitude and curiosity for fundamental research, usually demonstrated by early research achievements. PhD applicants should typically have publications.

  - Please check out the [research team page]({{ site.baseurl }}/team/research/) to see the type of students in my group and what they are working on.

- I cannot reply to every email I receive. I will only respond to students whom I have a strong interest in recruiting.

- If you decide to email me, please include:
  - Your resume (clearly showing GPA, GRE scores, awards, publications)
  - A brief explanation of why you are interested in working with me

- Please review the following resources for admission requirements and related information:

  - [U of A Faculty of Graduate Studies](http://www.gradstudies.ualberta.ca/){: target="_blank" }
  - [Graduate Studies in the Department of ECE](http://www.ece.engineering.ualberta.ca/Graduate.aspx){: target="_blank" }
  - [NSERC Postgraduate Scholarships](http://www.nserc-crsng.gc.ca/Students-Etudiants/PG-CS/index_eng.asp){: target="_blank" }
  - [Alberta Innovates Graduate Student Scholarship](http://www.albertatechfutures.ca/GSS.aspx){: target="_blank" }
  - [Alberta Innovates Technology Futures](http://www.albertatechfutures.ca){: target="_blank" }
