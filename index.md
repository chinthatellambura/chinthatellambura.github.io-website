<p>I am a Professor with the <a href="http://www.ece.ualberta.ca"
target="_blank">Department of Electrical and Computer
Engineering</a> at the <a href="http://www.ualberta.ca" target=
"_blank">University of Alberta</a>.</p>

<p>I received my BSc degree with first-class honors from the
University of Moratuwa, Sri-Lanka, in 1986, MSc from th
University of London, UK, in 1988, and PhD in Electrical
Engineering from the University of Victoria, Canada, in 1993. I
was a post-doctoral fellow with the University of Victoria
(1993-94) and the University of Bradford (1995-96). I was with
Monash University, Melbourne, Australia from 1997 to 2002. I served
as an associate Editor (modulation and signal design) for the <a href=
"http://www.comsoc.org/pubs/jrnal/transcom/home.html" target=
"_blank">IEEE Transactions on Communications</a> and&nbsp; an
area Editor for the <a href="http://www.comsoc.org/pubs/jrnal/twc.html" target=
"_blank">IEEE Transactions on Wireless Communications</a>.</p>

<p>I am interested in diverse topics in the general areas of Signal processing for wireless communications, Communication theory, and Information theory. Current and recent research projects involve some of the following subfields:</p>
<ul>
		<li>5G and Future Wireless Systems</li>
	    <li>Millimeter wave communications</li>
        <li>MIMO and  Massive MIMO</li>
        <li>Physical-layer network coding</li>
        <li>Machine Learning for communications</li>
	    <li>Stochastic geometry modelling</li>
        <li>Cooperative networks and relaying</li>
        <li>In-band full-duplex radio</li>
	    <li>Single- and multi-carrier systems</li>
        <li>Physical layer security</li>
        <li>Energy harvesting communication networks</li>
</ul>
