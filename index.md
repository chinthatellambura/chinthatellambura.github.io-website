I am a Professor with the [Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering](http://www.ece.ualberta.ca){:target="_blank"} at the [University of Alberta](http://www.ualberta.ca){:target="_blank"}.

I received my BSc degree with first-class honors from the University of Moratuwa, Sri Lanka, in 1986, MSc from the University of London, UK, in 1988, and PhD in Electrical Engineering from the University of Victoria, Canada, in 1993. I was a post-doctoral fellow with the University of Victoria (1993–94) and the University of Bradford (1995–96). I was with Monash University, Melbourne, Australia from 1997 to 2002. I served as an associate editor for the *IEEE Transactions on Communications* and an area editor for the *IEEE Transactions on Wireless Communications*.

I am interested in diverse topics in the general areas of Signal processing for wireless communications, Communication theory, and Information theory. Current and recent research projects involve some of the following subfields:
- 5G and Future Wireless Systems  
- Millimeter wave communications  
- MIMO and Massive MIMO  
- Physical-layer network coding  
- Machine Learning for communications  
- Stochastic geometry modelling  
- Cooperative networks and relaying  
- In-band full-duplex radio  
- Single- and multi-carrier systems  
- Physical layer security  
- Energy harvesting communication networks
